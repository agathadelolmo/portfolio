The trip that changed my life - June 2019

I was born and raised in a small town in Spain, surrounded by my family and friends, which made me feel so comfortable that I never felt the need to travel outside of Spain.

However, in the summer of 2019, I had the incredible opportunity to visit some relatives in the city of San Francisco, USA.

I toured Silicon Valley, visited the offices of Google, Facebook, and Glass, the University of Berkeley... Exploring such a technology-driven world up close sparked in me a new perspective on what I could achieve in the future: a real impact.

"Change begins at the end of your comfort zone."

- Roy T. Bennett

My first contact with programming - April 2021

In my first year of high school, I chose computer science as a subject. I learned the basics of SQL and hardware, but what really captured my interest was programming.

For the final project, I had to create a video game with Scratch. Just around that time, I contracted COVID, and during my isolation, I spent entire days working on it.

It was the most exciting work I did in high school. I stayed up until 4 a.m. perfecting every detail.

If you'd like to try it, here are some tips:

  • Use the arrow keys and space bar to select.
  • Press the m key to change the music and left-click to fish.
  • To enter a house, select the sign next to it.

The importance of a good teacher - May 2022

Interestingly, throughout high school, I was convinced that I was going to study history and become a teacher.

But on the day of my graduation, after receiving an Honors certificate, my economics teacher, Juan Carlos, told me about a new degree in Valencia.

He explained that it combined programming, economics, and data analysis, and that it had a technical focus that could complement any other discipline.

Since I have always trusted my teachers' wisdom, I researched the degree further... It was called Business Intelligence and Analytics (BIA).

The truth is that when I enrolled in the degree, I didn't understand what the subjects meant, but "data mining" sounded fun, and that, along with my intuition, was enough to convince me to sign up.
